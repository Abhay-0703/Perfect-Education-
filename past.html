Past Tense (अतीत काल)

Past Tense refers to actions or events that happened in the past. There are four main types of past tenses:

  • Past Simple (साधारण भूतकाल): Used for actions that happened at a specific point in the past.
    Structure: Subject + V2 (past form of the verb) + object.
    Example: I played football yesterday.
  • Past Continuous (अपूर्ण भूतकाल): Used for actions that were ongoing at a specific time in the past.
    Structure: Subject + was/were + V-ing (verb + ing) + object.
    Example: I was playing football when it started raining.
  • Past Perfect (पूर्ण भूतकाल): Used to describe an action that was completed before another action in the past.
    Structure: Subject + had + V3 (past participle) + object.
    Example: I had played football before he arrived.
  • Past Perfect Continuous (पूर्ण अपूर्ण भूतकाल): Used for actions that were ongoing for some time before another action happened.
    Structure: Subject + had been + V-ing (verb + ing) + object.
    Example: I had been playing football for two hours before it started raining.
